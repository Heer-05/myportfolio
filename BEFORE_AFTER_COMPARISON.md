## Design Philosophy Shift

### Before
- **Approach**: Featured showcase with supporting projects
- **Hierarchy**: One project emphasized, others secondary
- **Feel**: Portfolio grid with featured highlight
- **Aesthetic**: Dashboard-like with hierarchy
- **Composition**: Asymmetrical, unbalanced

### After
- **Approach**: Curated editorial showcase
- **Hierarchy**: All projects equally prominent
- **Feel**: Premium AI research gallery
- **Aesthetic**: Luxury editorial, balanced
- **Composition**: Symmetrical, refined

---

## User Experience Impact

### Before
- Users see one large featured project first
- Supporting projects feel secondary
- Asymmetrical layout creates visual tension
- Different card sizes confuse hierarchy
- Duplicate project (CogniFit) is redundant

### After
- All projects equally discoverable
- Balanced, editorial presentation
- Symmetrical layout feels refined
- Consistent card sizes create harmony
- No redundancy, clean showcase
- Premium, curated feel
- Better visual flow

## Summary

The redesign transforms the Projects section from an asymmetrical featured showcase into a clean, balanced 2×2 cinematic editorial grid. All four projects are now equally prominent, creating a premium, curated AI research showcase that feels sophisticated and refined.

**Key Achievement**: A truly balanced, editorial presentation that treats all projects with equal visual importance while maintaining cinematic atmosphere and luxury aesthetic.
